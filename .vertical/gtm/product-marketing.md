# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-08*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Vertical helps technical founders who build with coding agents get customers and grow their software businesses.

**What it does:** Vertical is exploring what it takes for agents to launch and operate software businesses. The current wedge is customer acquisition for builders who already use coding agents and have a product or early customers. Vertical focuses on the business context and GTM workflows around the products users build with tools like Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, and Lovable.

**Product category:** Agentic GTM, founder operating system, business context layer for AI-native builders.

**Product type:** Early SaaS / agent-first software product.

**Business model:** Currently early paid access at about EUR50/month per user. Final pricing is intentionally undecided and should follow willingness to pay, workflow depth, and measurable business outcomes.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Solo founders, technical founders, and small teams building PLG SaaS, developer tools, agencies, consulting products, content businesses, or other software-enabled businesses.

**Decision-makers:** Founder, technical founder, indie hacker, solo SaaS operator, AI-native builder.

**Primary use case:** Help founders turn an existing product into demand through customer research, positioning, lead generation, outreach, content, launch execution, and GTM follow-through.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Find the next customers after the product is built.
- Turn scattered business context into a usable operating memory.
- Run repeatable GTM experiments instead of manually guessing what to try next.
- Connect product work and customer acquisition into one learning loop.

**Use cases:**
- Define ICP and lead acquisition strategy.
- Generate and manage leads.
- Create outreach campaigns and follow up on responses.
- Build a content strategy and draft posts in the founder's voice.
- Track CRM notes, customer conversations, experiments, revenue, tasks, and product decisions.
- Help founders decide what to do next when growth feels unclear.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Product creation is getting commoditized by coding agents, but customer acquisition has not become 10x easier. Builders can ship faster than ever, then get stuck turning products into demand.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Coding agents help create software but do not own customer acquisition.
- GTM tools like Clay can execute workflows but do not understand the whole business loop.
- CRMs store contacts, not the reasoning and learning behind product and GTM decisions.
- Agencies can execute but do not compound inside the founder's operating context.
- Starting from zero creates too many variables; founders often get stuck before distribution matters.

**What it costs them:** Slow learning, unclear next steps, wasted product work, inconsistent outreach, weak positioning, missed revenue, and a sense that building is happening faster than the business is learning.

**Emotional tension:** The founder can build the product but still feels stuck, because the harder question is not "can I ship this?" but "can I get people to care?"

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Agentic GTM tools such as Polsia and adjacent AI sales/outreach products. They help execute GTM but may not connect deeply to product creation and business context.

**Secondary:** Clay, Apollo, HubSpot, Notion, Linear, email sequencing tools, social scheduling tools. These own pieces of the workflow but not the loop.

**Indirect:** Cursor, Claude Code, Codex, Lovable, GStack, Paperclip, Webflow, outbound agencies, manual founder-led sales and content.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Connects product creation, GTM, CRM, and business operations into one learning loop.
- Starts next to coding agents instead of competing with them.
- Treats persistent business context as a core primitive.
- Optimizes for business outcomes, not just task completion.
- Focuses on founders who already have product signal, not purely idea-stage users.

**How we do it differently:** Vertical aims to remember the business, act across GTM workflows, make agent reasoning legible, and use outcomes to improve future work.

**Why that's better:** If an agent can understand product, customers, conversations, experiments, revenue, and tasks together, it can learn faster than a collection of disconnected tools.

**Why customers choose us:** They want leverage on the business side that feels similar to the leverage they already get from coding agents.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| I already use coding agents. | Coding agents help you build. Vertical focuses on the loop after building: customers, demand, learning, and growth. |
| I can do outreach/content manually. | You can, but the work is inconsistent and context does not compound. The goal is a repeatable system. |
| Is this just another CRM? | No. CRM is one view. The deeper primitive is persistent business memory across product, customers, experiments, revenue, and tasks. |
| Is this too early or too vague? | The direction is clear; the exact wedge is still being learned through paid users and GTM experiments. |

**Anti-persona:** Idea-stage founders with no product, no customer signal, and no willingness to run real GTM experiments. Also founders who want fully polished agency work rather than an experimental agentic system.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Builders are shipping faster but still grinding to find customers. Existing tools are fragmented and do not learn from outcomes.

**Pull:** A system that understands the business and helps with leads, outreach, content, CRM, and GTM decisions.

**Habit:** Founders default to tweaking products, websites, and features instead of talking to customers or running distribution.

**Anxiety:** They may worry the agent will be generic, make mistakes, damage their voice, send bad outreach, or require too much setup.

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "I built something. Why doesn't anybody care?"
- "Getting the next 100 customers is harder than building the product."
- "I need help turning a product into demand."
- "I don't have an audience, a distribution engine, or a repeatable growth motion."
- "Building isn't the bottleneck anymore."

**How they describe us:**
- "Business leverage for founders using coding agents."
- "The GTM loop around my coding agent workflow."
- "A system that helps my business grow for me."

**Words to use:** feedback loop, learning loop, business context, outcomes, product to demand, agentic businesses, coding agents, GTM, customer acquisition, persistent memory, visibility, founder-led, experiments.

**Words to avoid:** generic AI automation claims, motivational founder content, vague "AI will change everything" language, over-polished mission statements, back-office automation as the main story.

**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Agentic business | A business where agents can increasingly build, operate, and learn across workflows |
| Business loop | The loop from idea/product to customers, outcomes, learning, and back to product |
| Persistent business memory | Durable context about products, customers, conversations, experiments, revenue, and tasks |
| Action layer | The ability for the agent to search, write, communicate, manipulate data, and execute workflows |
| Human-agent collaboration | The UI and interaction model that lets humans understand, trust, and steer agents |

## Brand Voice

**Personality:** Curious, rigorous, direct, slightly skeptical of narratives, optimistic about the direction but honest about uncertainty.
