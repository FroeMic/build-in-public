## Outline (~300 words total)

**1. The observation — where everyone is (~40w)**
- everyone talks about AI-native companies right now
- startups want to be it, VCs fund it, big companies commission strategies for it
- said as observed fact, no mockery

**2. The tangle — what struck me (~40w)**
- ask five people what it means, get five answers
- mostly it means "we use AI a lot"
- nobody has actually seen one — the thing everyone wants doesn't exist yet

**3. The belief — my definition (~60w)**
- what I think it will be: AI at the center, the central mechanism through
  which the company organizes
- not copilots at the edges helping people work faster
- work, context, and decisions routing through one intelligent layer;
  humans setting direction, taste, trust
- framed as belief ("I think", "the version I keep coming back to")

**4. Why I believe it — living in the future (~70w)**
- the best way to build the future is to live in it
- building with agents daily, you can see copilots are only the start:
  the same system that helps you write code can carry the context, the
  goals, the follow-through
- history beat (Azhar/Brynjolfsson, one sentence, plain words): every
  general-purpose technology paid off only after companies reorganized
  around it — electricity didn't change factories until the assembly line
- what that implied then, it implies now

**5. The honest state (~40w)**
- nobody has figured it out — not the startups claiming it, not us
- the tech isn't fully there; the org patterns aren't either
- everyone building today is guessing in the right direction

**6. The lingering line (~20w)**
- candidate: "the first AI-native companies won't be the ones that adopted
  AI. they'll be the ones organized around it. nobody has built one yet.
  that's the exciting part."
- shorter: "Everyone wants to be AI-native. It doesn't exist yet. That's
  the opportunity."
