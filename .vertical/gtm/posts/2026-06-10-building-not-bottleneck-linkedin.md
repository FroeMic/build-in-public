# Building Is No Longer The Bottleneck

Status: Draft
Platform: LinkedIn
Scheduled: 2026-06-10 15:00 Europe/Berlin
Pillar: Building vs Distribution

## Draft

I think the bottleneck for technical founders is shifting.

For a long time, the hardest part was building the product.

You needed engineering time, design time, infrastructure, and a lot of patience before you could even put something in front of users.

Coding agents are changing that.

They do not make product building easy. But they do make it much faster to get from idea to working software.

The strange consequence is that many founders can now build faster than they can learn from the market.

You can ship another feature.
You can rewrite the landing page.
You can improve the onboarding.
You can launch another small product.

But none of that answers the harder questions:

Who wants this?
How do we reach them?
What did they understand?
What did they ignore?
What made them pay?

The next layer of leverage for AI-native founders may not be more software creation.

It may be the loop after software creation:

Product -> market -> learning -> product.

That loop still feels underbuilt.
